# Residential Planning Heuristics

## Entry and Public Zone
Place the living room near the main entrance for intuitive circulation and visitor access.
Reserve the noisiest edge of the footprint for public functions and transition spaces.

## Kitchen and Service Logic
Keep the kitchen adjacent to dining and avoid long circulation paths for daily movement.
Prefer at least one external wall for ventilation and natural light.

## Bedroom Privacy
Locate bedrooms away from direct entry lines and major circulation corridors.
Stack wet areas where possible to simplify plumbing and maintenance.

## Stair and Vertical Core
Keep staircase placement central to reduce travel distance across floors.
Do not block primary public zone movement with stair landings.

## Ventilation and Openings
Distribute openings on more than one facade where possible for cross ventilation.
Avoid deep room proportions that reduce daylight penetration.

## Vastu Preference Guidance
Treat Vastu as a preference layer.
If Vastu and bylaws conflict, keep bylaws and safety constraints as higher priority.
